# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Recruiters and hiring managers evaluating Kevin Lin for a senior role or engagement. They usually arrive from LinkedIn, a resume, or a referral and scan fast, deciding in seconds whether this person is worth a conversation. A secondary audience of potential clients, partners, and tech peers follows his AI and engineering work and may land here from GitHub or the community.

## Product Purpose

A personal-brand site that establishes Kevin as a principal-level software architect and people leader, and moves the right visitor to start a conversation about working together. Success is a qualified recruiter reaching out, or leaving with the resume in hand.

## Positioning

Kevin bridges deep technical architecture and people leadership: he designs AI-powered enterprise systems and grows the teams that ship them.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: start a hire or work conversation (LinkedIn / GitHub connect). Secondary CTA: download the resume.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "Principal architect and people lead who turns emerging AI into enterprise-grade software, and builds the teams behind it."
- Belief ladder: (1) genuinely senior, principal-level architect; (2) leads and grows people, not only code; (3) works at the AI frontier and ships it in real enterprise settings; (4) the credentials are real and verifiable; (5) reaching out is easy and worth it.
- Proof on hand: Credly certifications (CKAD, AWS Solutions Architect, ForgeRock Access Management, SAFe, GovTech ICT Assessment for Solution Architect), GitHub activity and stats embeds, LinkedIn presence (1K+ followers, 500+ connections), and a downloadable resume.

## Brand Personality

Warm and human, confident and senior. The voice is a seasoned lead with nothing to prove: approachable, direct, plain-spoken, no hype.

## Anti-references

Should not read as a generic AI or template dev portfolio (gradient hero, identical icon-heading-text card grid, tracked uppercase eyebrows above every section). Not a flashy startup landing page (salesy tone, motion everywhere). Not a terminal or hacker aesthetic (dark mono, matrix costume). Not an overly corporate, stiff enterprise brochure with no personality.

## Design Principles

1. Substance over polish. Real credentials and real work carry the page; no hype.
2. People and systems together. Show both the architecture depth and the leadership, since the two are the differentiator.
3. Funnel to a conversation. Every section should make reaching out easier, not harder.
4. Distinctive, not templated. The page must never read as a generic AI portfolio.
5. Warm authority. Senior confidence paired with human warmth and plain language.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best-effort, with no formal WCAG target. Preserve the existing scaffolding (skip link, ARIA roles, keyboard navigation, light/dark theme toggle) and hold reasonable color contrast and reduced-motion support.
